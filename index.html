About

I'm not the person you give tasks to — I'm the person you give problems to. Here's how I think about problems and value.

How I think

I'm most comfortable when the brief is unclear. I take a messy situation, strip it down to first principles and rebuild it into a clear problem statement, options and trade-offs. I care about structured thinking and real user understanding more than flashy frameworks.

How I work

I move between research, conversation and documentation quickly: talk to users and stakeholders, pull the data that matters, map the flows, then turn it into a concise story that decision-makers can act on. I don't hide behind jargon or produce artefacts that go nowhere.

Where I'm useful

Roles with real ambiguity: UX research and design, product, and change initiatives that need someone to make sense of disconnected inputs, align stakeholders and push things over the line — including in regulated environments like banking.

Epicor — Making the System Understandable

ERP · User Journeys · Workflow Design

My role
ERP Analyst / UX-minded researcher
Problem
People used Epicor daily, but few could see how their actions flowed through inventory, production and reporting — causing avoidable errors and low adoption.
Process
Ran discovery sessions with operational users, mapped end-to-end user journeys, and turned them into process diagrams and user-focused documentation.

Outcome More intuitive workflows and shared diagrams that helped non-finance users understand the impact of their choices and adopt the system with confidence.

Research · Brand · FMCG

My role
Brand & Research Lead

Process
Structured a full-funnel audit across competition, consumer perceptions, retail presence and messaging using a simple but rigorous framework.

Outcome Clear strategic routes — defend, stretch or reposition — with concrete implications for pack, messaging and activation, instead of a generic brand-health report.
